## The picture to hold

Down on the plains — the bulk of the distribution — you can wander in any direction, and two
traits stay uncorrelated. *Many* trails climb toward the summit, and most who get high came up an
ordinary one, arriving with no particular kit and nothing in common. But a few trails carry
cargo, and everyone who comes up one of *those* arrives with the same load. You cannot pick the
cargo-carriers out by altitude alone — a lone climber high on one face might have taken any trail.
You spot them where the trails *converge*: the travelers who arrive high on several faces at once
came up a cargo trail, because no ordinary trail reaches more than one. The correlation was never
on the plain, and it isn't simply "at the top" — it lives at the *meeting point*, where extremes
coincide.

## The picture to hold

Picture trait space as high-dimensional — one axis per trait, every individual a point. In the
bulk, the cloud is diffuse: pick any two directions and they are uncorrelated, points scattered
every which way. Most of what reaches an extreme on *one* axis is just this cloud thinning out at
its edge — ordinary points that happened to land far along a single direction, sharing nothing
else.

A pathway is not a place in this space and not a road through it. It is a *low-dimensional
direction* — a thin thread running out toward the extremes along which a whole syndrome of traits
rises *together*. Travelers emitted by the same pathway land near that thread, so they are
**aligned**: high on many axes at once, and high in the *same combination*. They need never occupy
the same region, meet, or know of one another. The pathway is a direction they share, not a
destination they reach.

A *low-dimensional direction*, though, is by itself just a latent factor — and the literature
already has those. What earns the word **pathway** is the contrast with the *other* way a tight
pattern can appear at the extremes. A **filter** does not stretch the cloud along a direction; it
*clips* it — cuts a corner off with a selection boundary and keeps only what clears the gate. The
survivors in that corner look patterned too, but the pattern is *compensatory* and *closed*:
confined to the very axes the gate screened, where a shortfall on one trait must be bought with a
surplus on another, and lifting nothing on an axis nobody screened for. And it leaves a roster —
every point it cut away.

The thread leaves the opposite signature on every count: dependence that is *concordant* (the
syndrome rises together, with no trade-off), that reaches an **un-selected co-effect** (open, not
closed), and that leaves no roster, because the source *emitted* — it did not admit. So you do not
read a pathway off the alignment alone; a clipped corner can counterfeit alignment too. You read it
off *which kind*: a thread the cloud was stretched along, or a corner the cloud was clipped into.
The correlation was never in the bulk, and it is not merely "at the top" — it lives in the *kind of
alignment*, the signature a generative thread leaves and a filter cannot fake.
