# Axiom 1 — Extremes are reached by pathways, not by coincidence

A position far out on a trait's tail is a destination with very few roads to it. Occupying
that position is evidence of having traveled one of those roads.

The naive model treats an extreme value as a rare draw on a single axis — improbable, but
otherwise unremarkable. FTH treats it as a *destination*: reaching it implies a generating
process selective enough that very few routes arrive there at all.
