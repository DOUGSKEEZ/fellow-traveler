# Axiom 1 — Correlated extremes are reached by pathways, not by coincidence

A position far out on a single trait's tail has *many* roads to it — most of them ordinary,
uncorrelated draws that happened to land high. Occupying it is therefore only weak evidence of
having traveled a pathway. But a few of those roads are generative pathways, and they alone
arrive carrying a correlated syndrome. Occupying *several* extremes at once is strong evidence of
having traveled one of those few, because uncorrelated roads do not bundle — a second extreme
rides along only at the vanishing product of the two rates, never above it.

The naive model treats an extreme value as a rare draw on a single axis — improbable, but
otherwise unremarkable, and it is right that far: a single extreme *is* often just that. FTH's
claim is about the *conjunction*. A syndrome of co-occurring extremes is not a rare draw made
rarer; it is a *destination*, reachable only by a generating process selective enough to bundle
the traits — and very few routes bundle. The rarity is not in the altitude. It is in the cargo.
