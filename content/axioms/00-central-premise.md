# Central Premise

When you select an individual for an extreme value on some trait, you are not drawing a rare
*coordinate* — you are drawing *membership in the narrow set of pathways capable of reaching
that extreme*. Those pathways carry a syndrome of correlated traits. So conditioning on one
extreme makes other, seemingly unrelated extremes far more common than independence predicts.

The "impossible" co-occurrences at the tails are the visible signature of invisible shared
pathways.

## The picture to hold

Down on the plains — the bulk of the distribution — you can wander in any direction, and two
traits are uncorrelated. But there are only two or three trails to the summit. Everyone who
reaches the peak came up one of those few trails, so they arrive carrying nearly the same kit.
The correlation isn't on the plain. It's in the funnel near the top.
