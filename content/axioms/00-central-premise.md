# Central Premise

A single extreme tells you little on its own. Select one individual for one trait far out on its
tail and you may have drawn a *lucky bulk draw* — a rare, uncorrelated coordinate that merely
landed high — or a traveler who climbed a *generative pathway* carrying a whole syndrome of
correlated traits. From one axis you cannot tell which.

What cannot be faked is the *collision*. Condition on one extreme and watch the others: when
traits that are uncorrelated everywhere on the plain keep coming up extreme *together*,
independence is broken — two unrelated rarities do not co-occur above the product of their rates
by chance. The convergence of extremes is the tell that the cluster is no accident; something is
producing it.

But a converging cluster is still only a *surface*, and two different machines stamp out the same
surface. A **filter** that screened for those traits directly piles them together just as neatly
as a **pathway** that grew them together — the cluster alone cannot separate the two. Its
*fingerprint* can: a real pathway lifts an **un-selected co-effect** — some further trait nobody
screened for, carried up with the rest — and leaves no roster of the rejected, while a filter
leaves only the traits it selected on and a list of who failed. This premise asserts the pathway;
the demarcation is the instrument that earns it.

## The picture to hold

Down on the plains — the bulk of the distribution — you can wander in any direction, and two
traits stay uncorrelated. *Many* trails climb toward the summit, and most who get high came up an
ordinary one, arriving with no particular kit and nothing in common. But a few trails carry
cargo, and everyone who comes up one of *those* arrives with the same load. You cannot pick the
cargo-carriers out by altitude alone — a lone climber high on one face might have taken any trail.
You spot them where the trails *converge*: the travelers who arrive high on several faces at once
came up a cargo trail, because no ordinary trail reaches more than one. The correlation was never
on the plain, and it isn't simply "at the top" — it lives at the *meeting point*, where extremes
coincide.
