# Glossary

**Fellow Traveler** — a trait that travels with another not by coincidence but because both are
co-effects of the same generative pathway. The "companions you meet at the tail are precisely
those who traveled the same narrow path to get there."

**Pathway** — an endogenous, generative common cause (a fork) that produces a syndrome of
correlated traits. See `filter-vs-pathway`.

**Filter** — an exogenous, explicit selection gate (a collider). Produces only the spurious,
input-bound correlations of Berkson's paradox. See `filter-vs-pathway`.

**Collision** — the observation event: the point at which selection on an extreme drags the
joint tail into view. A pathway explains *why* the traits travel together; a collision is *why
we observe them together*. The two are distinct and stacked.

**Co-effect** — a trait that is an output of the pathway but was *not* an input to the
selection. The presence of a concordantly-elevated co-effect is the demarcation signature.

**Concordant dependence** — co-effects rise together (positive tail dependence), as opposed to
the compensatory (negative) trade-off that conditioning on a collider induces among its inputs.

**Tail dependence (λ)** — the probability that one variable is extreme given that another is.
λ → 0 in the bulk and under Gaussian dependence; λ > 0 at the extreme requires a heavy-tailed
copula, which a shared pathway supplies.

**Extraction** — the inverse application: given a pathway's emission signature, recover its
members by selecting on un-crowded co-effects rather than the overcrowded legible marker. The
*Moneyball* move. (To be developed in the derived layer.)

## Established prior art (named honestly, not claimed)

**Berkson's paradox / collider bias** — selection on a common effect induces a spurious
correlation among its causes. FTH's filter is exactly this. FTH's contribution is the
*demarcation* from it, not its discovery.

**Simpson's paradox** — an association that reverses or vanishes across aggregation levels.

**Copulas / extreme value theory** — the formal language of tail dependence (λ).
