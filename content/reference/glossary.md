# Glossary

**Fellow Traveler** — a trait that travels with another not by coincidence but because both are
co-effects of the same generative pathway. The "companions you meet at the tail are precisely
those who traveled the same narrow path to get there."

**Pathway** — an endogenous, generative common cause (a fork) that produces a syndrome of
correlated traits. See `filter-vs-pathway`.

**Filter** — an exogenous, explicit selection gate (a collider). Produces only the spurious,
input-bound correlations of Berkson's paradox. See `filter-vs-pathway`.

**Collision** — the observation event: the point at which selecting on an extreme drags the
joint tail into view. A pathway explains *why* the co-effects travel together; a collision is
*why we observe them together*. The two are distinct and stacked. The same word spans both
scales, deliberately: **intra-entity**, the colliding things are *traits* within one traveler
(the within-person syndrome); **inter-entity**, they are *travelers* within a population
(concordant individuals meeting where independence forbids). Using one word is not loose — it is
the claim that the two are one mechanism at two scales.

**Traveler** — an individual who reached an extreme by a pathway. The *co-occurring set* is the
travelers who collided — sized-agnostic and contact-agnostic: it may be **two** (a parallel-
discovery pair) or many (a cohort), **co-located** (one room) or **distributed** (different rooms,
no contact). The size and the co-location are separate axes from the collision itself; neither is
implied by the term. Note the deliberate inheritance: at the intra-entity scale the "fellow
travelers" are *traits*; at the inter-entity scale they are *people* — same role, different
substrate.

**Co-effect** — a trait that is an output of the pathway but was *not* an input to the
selection. The presence of a concordantly-elevated co-effect is the demarcation signature.

**Concordant dependence** — co-effects rise together (positive tail dependence), as opposed to
the compensatory (negative) trade-off that conditioning on a collider induces among its inputs.

**Tail dependence (λ)** — the probability that one variable is extreme given that another is.
λ → 0 in the bulk and under Gaussian dependence; λ > 0 at the extreme requires a heavy-tailed
copula, which a shared pathway supplies.

**Extraction** — the inverse application: given a pathway's emission signature, recover its
members by selecting on un-crowded co-effects rather than the overcrowded legible marker. The
*Moneyball* move. (To be developed in the derived layer.)

**Cluster (informal, deprecated for the precise claim)** — a largish, *co-located* set of
travelers. The word carries connotations of *many* and *clumped-together*, so it is reserved for
informal reference to sizeable co-located groups (a "scene," a "mafia") and is **never** used for
the precise claim, which is indifferent to size and to co-location. Use *collision* for the event
and *co-occurring set* for the people. Note that "clumped-together" is itself a contamination
signal: co-location is what contact-entanglement and covert-filter both require, so a "cluster" in
this informal sense is, by its own connotation, the *suspect* case — not the clean distributed one.

## Established prior art (named honestly, not claimed)

**Berkson's paradox / collider bias** — selection on a common effect induces a spurious
correlation among its causes. FTH's filter is exactly this. FTH's contribution is the
*demarcation* from it, not its discovery.

**Simpson's paradox** — an association that reverses or vanishes across aggregation levels.

**Copulas / extreme value theory** — the formal language of tail dependence (λ).
